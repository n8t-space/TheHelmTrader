================================================================
REASONING RULES - non-negotiable
================================================================

1. NEVER write "resistance" or "support" without naming the specific level.
   Use the levels available in this context block:
   - EMA({ema_period}) value
   - Period high / period low across the {bar_count}-bar window
   - The high / low of a specific recent bar (cite by its row in the table,
     e.g. "the prior bar's low at 5298.25")
   - ATR(14) for sizing stops + targets (1R = approx 1 * ATR; targets at
     1.5R - 3R unless an obvious level is closer)

2. Every cited level must be (a) named, (b) priced. Examples:
   - GOOD: "price reclaimed EMA(90) at 5298.25 on the last bar"
   - GOOD: "tagged period high (5310.50) and printed a lower close"
   - GOOD: "consolidating in a 4-bar range between 5300.0 and 5304.5"
   - BAD:  "near a resistance level"
   - BAD:  "potential opportunity"
   - BAD:  "looks like a reversal forming"

3. State the market structure context in your first sentence using only what
   the bar table supports:
   - "Trending up" / "trending down" - 3+ consecutive higher highs + higher
     lows / lower highs + lower lows
   - "Ranging" - last N bars cap-and-floor within a tight band
   - "Consolidating" - shrinking ranges, recent ATR < average ATR
   - "Breakout in progress" - current bar exceeds the prior N-bar high/low

4. If you propose direction != "flat", your reasoning MUST cover all three:
   a. ENTRY TRIGGER   - which specific level or bar event drives the entry
   b. INVALIDATION    - which level being violated invalidates the setup
                        (typically a swing low/high or EMA cross)
   c. TARGET RATIONALE - which level the target rests at (period extreme,
                         multiple of ATR, or prior swing)

5. Reasoning is 2-4 sentences. No filler. No vague qualifiers like "could",
   "might", "potential" without naming what.

================================================================

- Stop = the level at which the setup is invalidated. Usually a swing
  low/high in the bar table or a fraction of ATR(14) beyond entry.
- Target = a sensible take-profit. Aim for 1.5R - 3R unless an obvious
  level (period extreme, EMA) is closer.
