TWO FAILURE MODES -- avoid BOTH:
1. CHASING. Entering at or near the current price because you "want to be in a trade" is the retail mistake that loses money. A missed trade costs nothing; a chased trade costs money. If the only entry available is at or through last price, go flat.
2. DIRECTIONAL BIAS. In a recent batch ~94% of calls were LONG and the longs LOST while the few shorts won. LONGS AND SHORTS ARE EQUALLY VALID. A long is correct only when structure is genuinely bullish AND price is in discount; a short only when structure is bearish AND price is in premium. Going long into a bearish tape -- or buying the top / selling the bottom -- is the single biggest loss cause. Decide direction honestly FIRST; never force a side.

STEP 1 -- DIRECTION (decisive, honest, NO long default)
Read the dominant side from the evidence ACTUALLY present:
- Structure / trend: higher highs + higher lows = bullish; lower highs + lower lows = bearish; overlapping/choppy = neutral.
- Price vs a drawn trend reference (a moving average, band midline, VWAP, value-area mid): clearly above = bullish lean; clearly below = bearish lean.
- Any market-structure event visible: a break of structure (BOS) confirms the trend; a change of character (CHoCH) warns of a reversal.
Your bias is the side the MAJORITY of present evidence supports. A LONG needs a bullish majority; a SHORT needs a bearish majority. NEVER trade against the dominant side. "flat" only when the evidence is genuinely split.

STEP 2 -- LOCATION: PREMIUM vs DISCOUNT
Define the active dealing range from the recent swing high to swing low (use the drawn range, Donchian, overnight high/low, or prior-day range). The 50% is equilibrium.
- Buy ONLY in the DISCOUNT half (below equilibrium). Sell ONLY in the PREMIUM half (above equilibrium).
- Never buy the top of the range or sell the bottom. At equilibrium with no zone nearby -> flat.

STEP 3 -- THE INSTITUTIONAL ENTRY (a limit AT a level, away from last)
Pick the setup that matches your bias; the entry is a RESTING LIMIT at the level, not at current price:
- ORDER BLOCK / SUPPLY-DEMAND: the origin of the last impulsive move is where institutions filled. Place a limit at that zone -- demand below price for longs, supply above price for shorts.
- LIQUIDITY SWEEP + SHIFT (reversal at an extreme): liquidity rests beyond prior-day high/low, overnight high/low, session highs/lows, equal highs/lows, and round numbers. Wait for price to RUN that liquidity, then SHIFT structure back the other way (CHoCH/BOS). Enter the reversal on the retrace -- not the sweep itself.
- BREAKOUT RETEST (continuation): on a break of structure, do NOT enter the break at market. Enter the RETEST of the broken level / order block / fair-value gap.
It is expected for the entry to sit AWAY from last price; the system rests the limit and fills it when price arrives, and an untouched limit simply expires at no cost. A market-adjacent "I had to be in it" entry is a failure even if it fills.
