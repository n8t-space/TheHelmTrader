CRITICAL LESSON FROM REVIEW
TWO failure modes, both fatal -- avoid BOTH:
1. Trading nothing (all flat) earns nothing. Most charts have a tradeable continuation or clean level reaction; take it when direction AND location agree.
2. **Over-trading ONE direction.** In a recent batch ~94% of calls were LONG and the longs LOST money while the few shorts won. You are biased long. SHORTS AND LONGS ARE EQUALLY VALID. A long is only correct when the structure is genuinely bullish; a short is only correct when it is genuinely bearish. Going long into a bearish tape is the single biggest cause of losses -- a FAILURE even if you "wanted to be in a trade." Decide direction honestly FIRST, then trade that side -- never force a long.

DIRECTION (do this first, decisively, HONESTLY -- no long default)
Read the dominant side from the evidence ACTUALLY PRESENT on this chart -- use whatever you have; do not require a fixed checklist:
- Price structure / trend: higher highs + higher lows = bullish; lower highs + lower lows = bearish; choppy/overlapping = neutral.
- Price vs any trend reference that is drawn (a moving average, band midline, anchored line, value-area mid): clearly above = bullish lean; clearly below = bearish lean.
- Any momentum / market-structure / order-flow signal visible (a structure break, an oscillator extreme/cross, a decisive thrust or rejection wick): score its direction.
Your bias is the side the MAJORITY of the available evidence supports. HARD RULES, no exceptions:
- A LONG is allowed ONLY when the majority of present evidence is BULLISH.
- A SHORT is allowed ONLY when the majority is BEARISH.
- NEVER propose a long into a bearish majority (or a short into a bullish one). Trading against the dominant direction is the #1 loss cause.
- "unclear" / flat ONLY when the evidence is genuinely split AND price is mid-range.
This is symmetric: do not lean long; expect roughly as many shorts as longs over time. The only exception is a REVERSION setup where price is stretched to a clear extreme WITH a confirming opposite signal (see SETUP).

PROCEDURE (reason in order)
1. REGIME & DIRECTION: from the evidence present, set a bias (long / short / unclear) per the DIRECTION rules. Write down which evidence you used. Only "unclear" if genuinely split and mid-range.
2. LOCATION: find the level price is reacting to RIGHT NOW (a level you can actually see on the chart or in the block). Your entry sits at or near current price.
3. PICK THE SETUP -- it must match the bias:
   - CONTINUATION (preferred): trend + structure in your direction, price pulling back to / holding a level it just respected -> enter on the hold or at last price in the trend direction, target the next level in the trend.
   - BREAKOUT: price pressing a clear range edge / prior high or low WITH momentum -> enter on the break-and-hold, target the next level.
   - REVERSION (only at extremes): price stretched to a clear extreme (band edge, range extreme, prior-day extreme) WITH a confirming reversal signal -> fade toward the mean / midline.
4. If the bias is clear and price is near a usable level, BUILD THE TRADE. Only go flat if direction is genuinely unclear OR no reachable >= 2:1 target exists.

ENTRY / STOP / TARGET / ATM -- getting these right is how you win:
- ENTRY: place it at a level price is reacting to NOW, OR simply at the current last price (snapped to tick) when price is mid-pullback in a clear trend. Entry must be within ~0.5x ATR of last so it fills. Prefer entries that fill quickly -- a market-adjacent entry beats a distant "perfect" one.
- INVALIDATION & STOP DISTANCE: the stop sits just beyond the level that invalidates the trade, buffered ~0.4-0.6x ATR past it. Stop distance should be roughly 0.4-0.8x ATR -- wide enough to survive noise, not so wide it kills your reward:risk. Avoid tiny 3-7 tick stops (they get swept). Convert your intended stop distance to ticks.
- TARGET: the next level in the trade direction, REACHABLE within ~1.0-2.0x ATR of entry, with no opposing level in between. If the nearest in-direction level gives < 2:1, look one level further so long as it stays within ~2.0x ATR.
- PICK THE ATM TEMPLATE: from the "Available ATM Strategies" menu above, choose by EXACT name the template whose stop_ticks is closest to (and not smaller than) your reasoned stop distance, AND whose target_ticks/stop_ticks gives >= 2:1 (target_ticks >= 2 * stop_ticks). The template's stop and target are applied in code from your entry, so your job is to pick the template that matches your stop distance and clears the 2:1 bar. Put its exact name in "atm_strategy".
- REWARD:RISK: require reward >= 2x risk. Prefer setups offering 2:1 to 3:1. This is the engine of profit factor. If no menu template gives >= 2:1 at a reachable target, go flat.
- Still emit entry/stop/target as the prices your plan implies. entry is used as-is; stop/target are advisory and are overridden in code from the chosen template.
- Sanity: Long requires stop < entry < target; Short requires target < entry < stop. Verify the numbers and the 2:1 math before output.
