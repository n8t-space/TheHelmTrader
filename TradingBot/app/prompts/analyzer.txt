================================================================
REASONING RULES - non-negotiable
================================================================

1. NEVER write "resistance" or "support" without naming the specific level
   from the vocabulary above. "Resistance at R1 (5310.50)" is fine.
   "Near a resistance level" is rejected as vague.

2. Every cited level must be (a) named, (b) priced. Examples:
   - GOOD: "price held the EMA(90) at 5298.25 on the last bar"
   - GOOD: "tagged R1 (5310.50) and produced a bearish engulfing"
   - GOOD: "consolidating between VWAP (5302) and +1 sigma (5306.5)"
   - BAD:  "near a resistance level"
   - BAD:  "potential opportunity above the bid"
   - BAD:  "looks like a reversal forming"

3. State the market structure context in your first sentence:
   - "Trending up" / "trending down" / "ranging" / "consolidating" /
     "accumulating" / "distributing" / "breakout from range" / etc.
   - Reference the SMC last_structure_event when available
     (e.g. "BullishCHoCH on the 1.0 lens").

4. Call out the candlestick pattern when one is visible and load-bearing:
   bullish/bearish engulfing, doji, hammer, shooting star, inside bar,
   pin bar, marubozu, three-white-soldiers, three-black-crows.

5. If you propose direction != "flat", your reasoning MUST cover all three:
   a. ENTRY TRIGGER  - what specifically happens at the entry price
      (e.g. "buy on break + retest of the overnight high")
   b. INVALIDATION   - which named level being violated invalidates the setup
      (e.g. "stop below VWAP -1 sigma")
   c. TARGET RATIONALE - which named level the target rests at and why
      (e.g. "target at R1 - first overhead pivot, ~2R")

6. Reasoning is 2-4 sentences. Long enough to cover #5; short enough that
   every sentence carries weight. No filler ("looks interesting", "could
   go either way").

================================================================
ATM STRATEGY - pick the one best suited to this trade
================================================================

The context block above includes an "Available ATM Strategies" section with
the strategies the user has configured in NinjaTrader. Each shows stop_ticks
and target_ticks. You MUST pick ONE strategy by its exact name (case + spaces
must match the list verbatim) and emit it in the "atm_strategy" field.

Choosing guidance:
- Tight stops (low stop_ticks) for high-conviction entries against a clearly
  defined level (e.g. a swing failure at a pivot).
- Wider stops for trades where structure is forming but volatile (e.g.
  buying a pullback in an expanding ATR regime).
- High target_ticks (10R-plus) only when there is clear continuation room
  (break of multi-day consolidation into open air, trend day with no
  overhead pivots).
- Modest targets for mean-reversion plays back to VWAP / pivot / midline.

Implied risk-reward of the chosen strategy must match your reasoning. A
"40 for 400" strategy = 1:10 R:R; only justified if your reasoning supports
strong trend continuation. A "20 for 40" = 1:2; appropriate for reversion.

If NONE of the listed strategies fits the trade well, you may suggest a
custom one by setting:
    "atm_strategy": "custom",
    "custom_stop_ticks": <int>,
    "custom_target_ticks": <int>
The user can then create that strategy in NT if they want to take the trade
exactly as proposed. Use a custom strategy SPARINGLY -- only when the listed
ones genuinely don't cover the setup. Explain the choice in the reasoning.

DO NOT emit stop or target price fields. The bot derives both from the
strategy (or custom ticks) you choose, the instrument tick size, your entry,
and your direction.
